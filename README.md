# Korean Pansori Vocal & Narrative Traditions Registry

This repository preserves, in an immutable and decentralized format, the vocal, narrative, and performative traditions of **pansori** — Korea’s epic storytelling art recognized by UNESCO as Intangible Cultural Heritage.

Pansori is a synthesis of **sori** (sung vocalization), **aniri** (spoken narration), **ballim** (expressive gestures), and **chuimsae** (audience interjections), performed by a singer (*sorikkun*) accompanied by a drummer (*gosu*) playing the **buk**.

## Why Pansori Matters

Pansori represents:

- a **unique vocal discipline**, requiring years of training and vocal endurance  
- a **narrative tradition**, transmitting moral, social, and historical stories  
- a **regional lineage system**, with Dongpyeong, Seopyeong, and Jungpyeong schools  
- a **performative ritual**, involving audience participation through chuimsae  
- a **survivor of modernization**, preserved by master singers and apprentices  

Its five surviving repertoires — *Chunhyangga*, *Heungbuga*, *Jeokbyeokga*, *Sugungga*, and *Simcheongga* — form the core of Korea’s epic oral literature.

## Purpose of This Contract

The goal of this registry is to:

1. **Create a permanent, censorship-resistant record** of pansori traditions.  
2. **Preserve endangered vocal and narrative knowledge** with technical precision.  
3. **Document repertoires, techniques, gestures, and cultural context** in structured form.  
4. **Provide an immutable reference** for museums, researchers, performers, and future generations.  
5. **Honor the regional and stylistic diversity of pansori performance.**

The contract is intentionally:

- **ownerless** — no authority can alter or censor the registry  
- **payment-free** — no monetization, no barriers  
- **minimalistic and secure** — no external calls  
- **permanent** — entries become part of the cultural ledger  

## What Each Entry Records

- **repertoire** — Chunhyangga, Heungbuga, Jeokbyeokga, Sugungga, Simcheongga  
- **vocalTechniques** — sori, aniri, chuimsae  
- **gestures** — ballim, expressive movement  
- **region** — Jeolla, Gyeongsang, Seoul  
- **accompaniment** — buk rhythmic cycles (jangdan)  
- **culturalContext** — performances, rituals, training lineages  
- **uniqueness** — UNESCO status, regional schools
